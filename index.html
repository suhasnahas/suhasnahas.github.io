I am condensed matter/materials theorist, who work in the physics of "Dirac Materials" like graphene, topological insulators, weyl semi-metals and related topological phases of materials. "Dirac materials" exhibit numerous novel and exciting phenomena, with lot of open problems most of them of fundamental relevance yet to be uncovered. These materials have close connections with the idea of emergence (Thanks to Philip. W. Anderson and Robert Laughlin for their articles "More is Different" and "A Different Universe: Reinventing Physics from the Bottom Down" ) where in many cases, their behaviour (e.g. quasiparticle excitations) are seen to follow directly from equations developed for elementary particles. Techonlogically, they are considered to be ideal candidates for implementing a variety of emerging technologies of the post-silicon era; ranging from devices for overcoming energy/environmental issues to materializing the dream idea of quantum computing.

I recently graduated from the Department of Materials science and Engineering of IIT Kanpur(2013-2015) and is currently working as a project engineer in the same department. My postgraduate thesis was on the "First Principles prediction of the structure of Amorphous Semiconductors using Evolutionary algorithms" under the guidance of Dr. Somnath Bhowmick and Dr. Anshu Gaur. Apart from my thesis, I got involve in exciting problems related to the physics of low dimensional materials in collaboration with Dr. Amit Agarwal from the Department of Physics. Prior to my postgraduation, I did my undergraduation from the department of Mechanical Engineering in National Institute of Technology(NIT)(2009-2013), Calicut, and schooling from St. Antony's Public School and Junior College, Kanjirapally (2007-2009) and Infant Jesus Anglo-Indian Higher Secondary school, Kollam (1998-2007)
