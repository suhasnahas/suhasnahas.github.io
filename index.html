I am a condensed matter/materials theorist, who studies the physics of "Dirac Materials" like graphene, topological insulators, weyl semi-metals and other topological phases of materials. These materials exhibit numerous novel and exciting phenomena, and many problems of fundamental relevance related to these materials are yet to be resolved. The physics of these materials have close connections with the idea of emergence (Thanks to Philip. W. Anderson and Robert Laughlin for their articles "More is Different" and "A Different Universe: Reinventing Physics from the Bottom Down" ) and in many cases are analogous to that of individual elementary particles (e.g. quasi-particle excitations in these materials follow the linear-dispersion shown by relativistic fermions). Technologically, they are considered to be ideal candidates for implementing a variety of emerging technologies of the post-silicon era; ranging from devices related to energy and environmental issues to materializing the dream idea of quantum computing.

I recently graduated from the Department of Materials science and Engineering of Indian Institute of Technology, Kanpur (2013-2015) and is currently working as a project engineer in the same department. My postgraduate thesis was on the "First Principles prediction of the structure of Amorphous Semiconductors using Evolutionary algorithms" under the guidance of Dr. Somnath Bhowmick and Dr. Anshu Gaur. Apart from my thesis, I got involved in exciting problems related to the physics of low dimensional materials in collaboration with Dr. Amit Agarwal from the Department of Physics. Prior to my post-graduation, I did my under-graduation from the department of Mechanical Engineering in National Institute of Technology, Calicut (2009-2013) and schooling from St. Antony's Public School and Junior College, Kanjirapally (2007-2009) and Infant Jesus Anglo-Indian Higher Secondary school, Kollam (1998-2007)
