I am a materials/condensed-matters theorist, who works in the physics of strongly correlated materials (Transition metal oxides) and Dirac Materials (graphene, topological insulators, weyl semi-metals and related topological phases). This particular interest, above all is due to the numerous emergent phenomena (Thanks to Philip. W. Anderson and Robert Laughlin for their articles "More is Different" and "A Different Universe: Reinventing Physics from the Bottom Down" respectively) exhibited by these materials. Besides being exciting from an emergent point of view, there are yet a lot of physics with fundamental relevance to be uncovered in these materials. These materials are further considered to be one of the most crucial class of materials in many of the emerging technologies of the post-silicon era. The concept of emergence is actually a subject of broader interest to me even outside the domains of physics as in open-source software, wiki projects and in many general systems where a spontaneous order is observed.

I recently graduated from the Department of Materials science and Engineering of Indian Institute of Technology, Kanpur (2013-2015) and is currently working as a project engineer in the same department. My postgraduate thesis was on the "First Principles prediction of the structure of Amorphous Semiconductors using Evolutionary algorithms" under the guidance of Dr. Somnath Bhowmick and Dr. Anshu Gaur. Apart from my thesis, I got involved in exciting problems related to the physics of low dimensional materials in collaboration with Dr. Amit Agarwal from the Department of Physics. Prior to my post-graduation, I did my under-graduation from the department of Mechanical Engineering in National Institute of Technology, Calicut (2009-2013) and schooling from St. Antony's Public School and Junior College, Kanjirapally (2007-2009) and Infant Jesus Anglo-Indian Higher Secondary school, Kollam (1998-2007)
